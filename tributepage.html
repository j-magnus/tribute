"It's a top selling author. Do we want that in here?"

Portland’s Women and Women First feminist bookstore, run by eccentric duo Toni and Candace, specializes in feminist literature, community, and female sexuality. The store, featured in “Portlandia”, runs into many events as visitors come through the doors, often introducing toxic masculinity and the oppressive male gaze. From a Steve Buscemi look-a-like who doesn’t honor store code, to ladies trips to save a flooding river, the iconic duo never stop educating Portland on the feminist agenda.

Toni and Candace pioneered Portland’s feminist movement and fight for female representation and equality. Their friendship and bond of womanhood was a beacon of beautiful rage. No man could penetrate the interior space of female celebration they had created within their store and community. It was a power that blazed through Portland like the ripples of a labia. After eight long years, the queens retired and passed on their power to the next generation of females at Women and Women First, the feminist bookstore.
